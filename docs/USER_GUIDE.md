# DocuChat AI - User Guide

Welcome to **DocuChat AI**! This guide will help you get the most out of your multi-document assistant.

## 🌟 Introduction
DocuChat AI allows you to "talk" to your PDF documents. Instead of searching through hundreds of pages, you can simply ask questions and get answers with direct references to where the information was found.

## 🛠 How to Use

### 1. Uploading Documents
- Click the **Upload** button or drag and drop your PDFs into the sidebar.
- You can upload up to **10 files** per session.
- Each file must be under **10MB**.
- **Note:** Only text-based PDFs are supported. Scanned images (OCR) are not currently supported.

### 2. Asking Questions
Type your question in the chat box at the bottom. For best results:
- **Be Specific**: Instead of "What does it say about money?", try "What are the payment terms in the Service Agreement?"
- **Context Matters**: If you have multiple files, specify which one you are interested in if needed, though the AI searches all of them by default.

### 3. Reading Citations
Every answer provided by the AI will include citations like this: `[Project_Plan.pdf - Page 4]`. 
- This tells you exactly which file and which page the information came from.
- If the AI says "The answer was not found in the uploaded documents," it means the information is not present in your files.

### 4. Managing Your Session
- **Delete Files**: You can remove specific files by clicking the 'X' next to the filename in the sidebar.
- **Clear Chat**: To start a completely fresh session and wipe all uploaded data from the server, use the "Clear Session" or "New Chat" button.

## ⚠️ Important Limitations
- **No Hallucinations**: The AI is strictly programmed to only answer based on your files. It will not use outside knowledge to answer questions.
- **Text Only**: If your PDF is a scan of a physical paper without selectable text, the AI will not be able to read it.
- **Session Based**: Your files are stored temporarily for your session. If you close the browser or clear your cache, you may need to re-upload your documents.

## ❓ Troubleshooting
- **Upload Failed**: Ensure the file is a PDF and under 10MB.
- **Slow Responses**: Large documents take a few seconds to process and embed. Please wait for the "Ready" indicator.
- **Incorrect Page Numbers**: Page numbers refer to the PDF's internal numbering, which may differ slightly from printed page numbers if there are cover pages.
